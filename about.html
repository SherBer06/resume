A freaking house. It was a strong, well planned, beautiful home. No contractor. Just us. It was AWESOME and we learned so much working together. It was so challenging but we loved every minute....minus the 15 where we fought over where the pendant lights should hang.

Although building a home was fun, our greatest accomplishments are our three kids. Two boys and one girl. Our daughter has a rare genetic disease called Cystic Fibrosis. She was diagnosed at 10 days old and since then, she's had thousands of hours of therapy and even more doses of medications. Too many doctor visits, hospital visits and labs to count. She is our little hero and her older brothers are our champions. They are awesome kids!

Most importantly is what I've learned from her diagnosis and the juggling act that has followed. Balancing life while managing the circus that has become our lives takes creativity and grit. I've always loved organizing (like since I was 3) and I really enjoy problem solving. I'm a hard worker and like to get things done. My worst quality is that I don't like to ask for help. I'm definitely up for a challenge and can't wait to see where this new life in the tech world will take me.
